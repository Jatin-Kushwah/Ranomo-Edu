Canada is one of the most popular study abroad destinations for Indian students — and for good reason. Beyond its excellent universities and PR pathways, Canada offers substantial scholarship opportunities specifically for international students. Here are the top 5 you should know about.

## 1. Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships

**Value:** CAD 50,000/year for 3 years  
**Level:** PhD programs  
**Eligibility:** Nominated by your Canadian university; exceptional academic achievement + research potential + leadership

The Vanier CGS is the most prestigious scholarship in Canada for doctoral students. Very competitive — only ~167 scholarships are awarded annually across all disciplines.

## 2. Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS)

**Value:** CAD 5,000 – CAD 15,000 per year  
**Level:** Master's and PhD  
**Eligibility:** Enrolled at an Ontario university; minimum 80% average (A grade)

OGS scholarships are funded jointly by the Ontario government and universities. Each university manages its own allocation, so application processes vary — check your specific university's deadline.

## 3. University-Specific Merit Scholarships

Most Canadian universities offer automatic merit scholarships ranging from CAD 2,000 to CAD 20,000 based on your academic grades. Notable examples:

- **University of Toronto**: National Scholarship (up to CAD 7,500/year for undergrads with >95%)
- **UBC**: International Leader of Tomorrow (up to full tuition for exceptional undergrads)
- **McGill**: Entrance Scholarship (up to CAD 10,000 for high achievers)
- **University of Waterloo**: International Excellence Award (varies by program)

## 4. Trudeau Foundation Scholarship

**Value:** Up to CAD 60,000/year  
**Level:** PhD only  
**Eligibility:** Social sciences and humanities doctoral candidates; must be nominated by your university

One of Canada's most prestigious awards, focusing on research with social impact. Applications are highly competitive and require a strong research proposal.

## 5. IDRC Research Awards

**Value:** CAD 15,000 – CAD 30,000  
**Level:** Master's and PhD  
**Eligibility:** Research focused on international development challenges affecting low/middle-income countries

IDRC awards are ideal for Indian students researching topics like sustainable agriculture, public health, or digital economy — areas with direct relevance to India's challenges.

## Tips for Maximizing Your Scholarship Chances

- **Start early**: Most scholarship deadlines are November–February for September intake
- **Maintain a strong GPA**: Most merit scholarships have a minimum 80% requirement
- **Write a compelling research statement**: Even for non-research programs, a strong statement of purpose helps
- **Apply broadly**: Apply to all scholarships you're eligible for — stacking smaller awards is common
