Getting a UK Student visa (formerly known as the Tier 4 visa) is a multi-step process — but with the right preparation, it's very achievable. Here's everything you need to know for 2026 applications.

## Step 1: Receive Your Unconditional Offer

Before you can apply for a visa, you need an **unconditional offer** from a UK university that is a UKVI-licensed Tier 4 sponsor. Once you accept, your university will issue a **CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies)** number — the most important piece of your visa application.

## Step 2: Gather Your Documents

You'll need:

- **Valid passport** (at least 6 months validity beyond your course start date)
- **CAS number** from your university
- **Proof of English proficiency** (IELTS 6.0+ or approved equivalent)
- **Financial evidence** — you must show you can cover tuition + £1,334/month for living costs (£1,023/month if studying outside London) for up to 9 months
- **Academic transcripts** and degree certificates
- **ATAS certificate** (if your subject is on the ATAS list — most STEM subjects at postgraduate level)

## Step 3: Apply Online

Apply through the [UK Visas and Immigration (UKVI) online portal](https://www.gov.uk/student-visa). You can apply up to **6 months before your course starts**.

Application fee: **£490** for applications outside the UK. You'll also pay the **Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)**: £776/year for students.

## Step 4: Book Your Biometrics Appointment

After submitting your online application, book a biometrics appointment at a **Visa Application Centre (VAC)** in your home country. Bring all original documents.

## Step 5: Wait for a Decision

Standard processing: **3–8 weeks** from your biometrics appointment. Priority service (where available) can reduce this to 5 working days for an additional fee.

## Common Reasons for Refusal

- Insufficient financial evidence (most common reason)
- Course not related to previous study or work history
- Gaps in education not explained in the application
- University not on the UKVI sponsor list

## Tips for a Successful Application

1. Apply early — give yourself at least 3 months before your course start date
2. Keep financial documents in one bank account for at least 28 consecutive days
3. Get a detailed SOP addressing your study-work gap if applicable
4. Check the ATAS requirement for your specific subject code
